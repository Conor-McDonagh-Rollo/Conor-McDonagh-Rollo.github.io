A website made for the worlds greatest role playing game.

What is D&D?

The core of D&D is storytelling. You and your friends tell a story together, guiding your heroes through quests for treasure, battles with deadly foes, daring rescues, courtly intrigue, and much more. You can also explore the world of Dungeons & Dragons through any of the novels written by its fantasy authors, as well as engaging board games and immersive video games. All of these stories are part of D&D.[1]

For a lot of people, 'Dungeons & Dragons' has been a hard thing to describe. I can't tell you how many social environments I've been in where I say, 'I play 'D&D,'' and a bunch of normies will be like, 'How does the game even work? What's that like?' I didn't have anything to really describe it that didn't make me sound like a crazy person. - Matthew Mercer

How can I play D&D?

Sadly D&D requires friends so that means half of the people reading this can leave. Now that only people with friends are left, you're going to want to talk to them and coerce them into joining you on your adventure. Now that you have friends who are interested, you will need to appoint someone the role of Dungeon Master.
A Dungeon Master is someone who will organize the game and create the details and challenges of the adventure. They are the referee and the storyteller. Since the rules of D&D are vast and complex, the Dungeon Master (which is abbreviated to 'DM') is to facilitate the gameplay and to determine the outcome of events by deciding how to interpret a given rule or dice roll.

History of D&D

Dungeons and Dragons was a game that was created by a man in a basement who couldn't find a publisher. His name was Garry Gygax and with a few other people, he developed a game called Chainmail which was later renamed to AD&D (Advanced Dungeons & Dragons). He borrowed heavily from the setting of Tolkien's "Middle Earth" and the game rose to popularity. However this came with controversy as in the 1980s to the 1990s, there was a lot of absurd claims from christian religious groups who claimed that D&D was a game about satanic rituals, so they did everything in their power to stop it from rising. What fueled the fire of the christian claims was the hysteria of the 16th and 17th witch hunts and McCarthyism paranoia. With the religious objections and apparent psychological impact; the adults and children who played this game were baselessly punished, shamed and ostracized.
Thankfully coming into the second half of the 90s, everything calmed down and it became ok to play D&D even if you did get bullied for being a nerd. However now many years later, people who come from all walks of life enjoy D&D, including many that aren't nerds.

What will I need?

For D&D you will only need a few things, which are all actually optional.

Paper

For your character sheets you need paper so that you can print them off. You can find a printable character sheets here.

Dice

For D&D, you are required to use some specific types of dice. These include a D4(4 sided), D6(6 sided), D8(8 sided), D10(10 sided), D12(12 sided), D20(20 sided), D100(variation of the D10). You can find these online or in a local gaming store.

Player Handbook

The player handbook is pretty much essential for at least the DM to have. It contains all the core rules as well as information on how to create characters, npcs, items etc. You can get it on Amazon here.

However you don't actually need any of this nowadays to play the game. If you have a phone, you're set. Roll your dice on google, there are many apps for managing characters and the players handbook is pretty much available anywhere in many different forms. If you aren't in the same country as the people you want to play with, then you can use online tabletop websites such as Roll20. This is why D&D is such an amazing game.
